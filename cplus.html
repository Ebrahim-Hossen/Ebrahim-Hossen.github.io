Dennis Ritchie created C, one of the most important programming languages in computer history, at Bell Labs in the early 1970s. Its initial purpose was to advance the UNIX operating system. Because of its ease of use, effectiveness, and portability, C gained immense popularity and became a mainstay in software development for a wide range of platforms. The language is appropriate for system programming, application development, and all in between since its high-level structures provide abstraction and flexibility, while its low-level capabilities allow for direct hardware manipulation. Beyond its own capabilities, C has influenced numerous other languages, such as C++, Java, and Python, by acting as their basis. Its resilience, adaptability, and broad use have contributed to its lasting impact and cemented its place as a fundamental component of contemporary computing. Bjarne Stroustrup created C++, a potent object-oriented programming language, at Bell Labs in the early 1980s. It expands upon C, its predecessor. Classes, inheritance, polymorphism, encapsulation, and other object-oriented characteristics made C++ a flexible and expressive language appropriate for a broad variety of applications. Building high-performance software nowadays is still often done with C++ in a variety of fields, such as operating systems, embedded systems, game development, scientific computing, and more. Because of its efficiency, versatility, and resilience, it is still the language of choice for developers all around the world.

Predefined terms with particular connotations for the compiler are called keywords.For example : 


                       int salary;

Here, int is a keyword that indicates salary is a variable of type integer.

Identifiers are the unique names given to variables, classes, functions, or other entities by the programmer. For example, 


                       int salary;

Here, salary is a identifiers.
Rules for naming identifiers :
1. Identifiers can be composed of letters, digits, and the underscore character.
2. It has no limit on name length.
3. It must begin with either a letter or an underscore.
4. It is case-sensitive.
5. We cannot use keywords as identifiers.
